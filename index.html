I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Graduate School of Artificial Intelligence at KAIST, advised by Prof. Kijung Shin. I am broadly interested in graph mining and machine learning on graphs, with a particular focus on higher-order networks (hypergraphs) — a powerful abstraction for modeling real-world group interactions that go beyond pairwise relations.

My work spans scalable generative models (HyRec, MiDaS, Kronecker models), hypergraph neural networks (WHATsNET), and LLM-integrated inference systems, aiming to make higher-order network analysis both theoretically principled and practically deployable. I have published at KDD, WWW, ICDM, IJCAI, and TKDD, and received the Best Paper Award at ICDM 2025.

I have interned at NEC Laboratories America and conducted a visiting research stay at Carnegie Mellon University with Prof. Christos Faloutsos. I am currently collaborating with Samsung on early-stage R&D automation.
