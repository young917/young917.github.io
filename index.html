I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Graduate School of Artificial Intelligence at KAIST, advised by Prof. Kijung Shin. My research focuses on graph mining and machine learning on graphs, with a particular interest in higher-order networks (hypergraphs) — structures that capture group interactions beyond pairwise relations.

My work spans scalable generative models (MiDaS, Kronecker models), hypergraph neural networks (WHATsNET), and LLM-integrated inference systems. I have published at KDD, WWW, ICDM, IJCAI, and TKDD.

Recently, I have become increasingly interested in knowledge graphs — in particular, how structured knowledge can enhance the reasoning capabilities of large language models. I am also broadly interested in multi-agent systems, and am currently exploring their application to tasks such as automated knowledge graph construction from scientific literature.
