About Me

     
       
         

            I am a Ph.D. candidate at KAIST working on hypergraph mining —             a field that studies how groups, not just pairs, interact in the real world.             What drew me to this area is the gap between how we usually model data             (edges between two nodes) and how interactions actually happen             (emails with multiple recipients, papers with multiple authors, reactions with multiple reagents).             Closing that gap, rigorously and at scale, is what I find deeply satisfying.          

         

            One thing people notice about me is that I tend not to let go of a problem.             Whether it is a stubborn proof or a pipeline that refuses to converge,             I find myself wanting to understand why before moving on.             That stubbornness has occasionally been useful.          

         

            Outside of research, I play jazz piano.             Jazz taught me that structure and improvisation are not opposites —             you need both to make something interesting.             I try to bring the same balance to research.

Research

     

        Real-world systems — co-authorship, email threads, Q&A platforms — involve         group interactions that pairwise graphs cannot faithfully represent.         Hypergraphs generalize graphs by allowing edges over any number of nodes.         My thesis, Generation and Inference of Real-world Hypergraphs, addresses two questions:         how to generate realistic hypergraphs and         how hypergraph structure can improve inference.         I am drawn to problems where structural elegance meets real-world impact —         from weather forecasting to automated scientific discovery.      

     
       
         
Hypergraph Generation
         
Scalable generative models that reproduce structural and evolutionary patterns via Kronecker self-similarity and degree-aware sampling.
         
            HyRec · WWW'25             MiDaS · WWW'22             HyperLAP · WWW'21          
       
       
         
Hypergraph Inference
         
Hypergraph neural networks for edge-dependent node classification, and LLM agents guided by hypergraph-structured knowledge.
         
            WHATsNET · KDD'23             Proposed: HG-Agent          
       
       
         
Applied Research
         
Deploying graph and AI methods in practice: satellite video retrieval at scale and multi-agent ML automation pipelines.
         
            SkySearch · KDD'25 Industry          
       
       
         
Scientific AI
